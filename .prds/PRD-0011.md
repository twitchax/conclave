# Summary

Round out the CLI so operators can audit every durable moderation state (bans, invites — completing
the acl-list work), debug identity at a glance (`status`), and participate as humans (`send`,
`tail`) — plus the small polish items surfaced during live deployment testing (leave_channel tool,
completions, a docstring bug). Ships as v0.2.1.

# Problem

Live testing surfaced write-only state and missing ergonomics: durable bans with no list/unban
verb, invites auditable only via kept tokens, no "who am I" view, no way for a human to watch or
post to a channel without a Claude session, and sessions that cannot unsubscribe without
disconnecting.

# Goals

1. No write-only moderation state: bans and invites are listable by their admins.
2. `unban` is first-class, not an acl-add side effect.
3. One-command identity/connectivity debugging (`status`).
4. Human channel participation from the plain CLI (`send`, `tail`).
5. Ship it: skill/docs current, v0.2.1 released and deployed.

# Technical Approach

Wire additions are append-only `AdminOp` variants; `BanList` reuses the `UserList` response,
`InviteList` adds a purpose-built frame. Hub arms follow the `authorize_channel_admin` pattern from
acl-list. `tail` is a small persistent loop in the control client (select over inbound frames and a
keepalive ticker) — deliberately not the bridge, which stays MCP-only. `status` composes existing
pieces (config, perm rendering, one-shot Who probes with the PRD-0008 timeouts).

# Assumptions

- The deployed server is updated (fly deploy) before the new CLI verbs are exercised against it.
- Channel-scoped ban/invite listing covers the operator need; server-wide sweeps can come later.

# Constraints

- Append-only wire evolution (protocol §13); no breaking CLI-surface changes (`ban` keeps its
  current positional form; `unban`/`bans` are new verbs).
- Constitution §8: every verb/tool ships a test; e2e spawns the real binary.

# Non-Goals (MVP)

- Server-wide (cross-channel) ban/invite sweeps and `--json` output — later if needed.
- The full §19 aggregation TUI — `tail` is the minimal version.
- Rate limiting / registration gating (deliberately kept open per the 2026-07-03 decision).
